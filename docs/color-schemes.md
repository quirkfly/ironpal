# IronPal — Brand Color Schemes

## Current Brand Baseline

The existing IronPal product identity uses **matte black** hardware with **electric teal** accents — printed logo, LED indicator, UI highlights. This document proposes three complete color schemes that extend from the product into all marketing materials: Kickstarter page, social media, video overlays, email campaigns, packaging, and investor decks.

Each scheme defines a primary, secondary, accent, background, and text palette. All three are designed to feel modern, premium, and fitness-aligned while keeping the physical product's matte-black-with-teal identity as the anchor.

---

## Color Scheme 1 — "Stealth Teal" (Dark Minimal)

### Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Charcoal Black | `#1A1A2E` | Page backgrounds, hero sections, video backgrounds |
| **Secondary** | Gunmetal Gray | `#2D2D3A` | Card backgrounds, secondary panels, dividers |
| **Accent** | Electric Teal | `#00E5CC` | CTAs, highlights, progress bars, active states, LED glow |
| **Accent 2** | Ice White | `#F0F4F8` | Headings, primary text on dark backgrounds |
| **Neutral** | Slate Gray | `#8E8E9A` | Body text, captions, secondary labels |
| **Alert/Energy** | Neon Lime | `#BFFF00` | Sparingly — sale badges, urgency indicators, limited CTA variants |

### Visual Identity

- **Dominant feel:** Dark-mode everything. The marketing materials live in a near-black world where teal is the only color that "lights up," mimicking the product's teal LED glowing in a dark gym.
- **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans, or Satoshi). Headings in Ice White, body in Slate Gray. No serif fonts — this is tech, not editorial.
- **Photography treatment:** Gym photos desaturated to 60-70% and given a cool blue-gray tint. The IronPal product (headband/cap) and its teal LED remain at full saturation — making the product visually pop in every image.
- **Gradients:** Subtle dark gradients from `#1A1A2E` → `#2D2D3A` for depth. Teal-to-transparent radial glows behind product shots.

### Sample Applications

- **Kickstarter hero banner:** Full dark background, IronPal headband and cap floating in center with dramatic side lighting, teal LED glowing, teal "Back this project" CTA button.
- **Social media post:** Dark card with desaturated gym photo, athlete in headband, teal text overlay: "Your workout. Logged automatically."
- **Video end card:** Pure `#1A1A2E` background, IronPal logo in `#00E5CC`, tagline in `#F0F4F8`.

## Color Scheme 2 — "Iron & Ember" (Warm Contrast)

### Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Deep Iron | `#121212` | Page backgrounds, hero sections |
| **Secondary** | Warm Charcoal | `#1E1E1E` | Card backgrounds, panels |
| **Accent** | Ember Orange | `#FF6B35` | CTAs, highlights, energy accents, rep counters |
| **Accent 2** | Electric Teal | `#00E5CC` | Product-specific elements — LED, logo, app UI |
| **Neutral Light** | Warm White | `#FAF0E6` | Headings, primary text |
| **Neutral Mid** | Ash Gray | `#9A9A8E` | Body text, captions |

### Visual Identity

- **Dominant feel:** The warmth of iron and fire meets the cool precision of tech. Ember Orange is the energy color — it represents effort, heat, and movement. Electric Teal stays reserved for the product itself, creating a clear visual association: teal = IronPal product; orange = human energy.
- **Typography:** Bold, slightly condensed sans-serif for headings (Bebas Neue, Oswald, or Barlow Condensed). Regular weight sans-serif for body (Inter or Source Sans). The condensed headings give an athletic-poster feel.
- **Photography treatment:** Warm golden-amber lighting on gym shots. High contrast with deep shadows. Skin tones warm and rich. The aesthetic references high-end sports photography (Nike "Just Do It" campaign tone).
- **Gradients:** Warm dark gradients (`#121212` → `#1E1E1E`). Ember Orange radial glow behind CTA buttons. Subtle teal glow on product shots only.

### Sample Applications

- **Kickstarter hero banner:** Dark background, athlete mid-squat with headband, warm amber sidelight on skin, teal LED on headband glowing, ember orange "Back Us Now" CTA button.
- **Social media post:** Warm-toned gym photo, orange kinetic typography overlay: "3 sets. 12 reps. 80 kg. Logged in zero taps." Small teal IronPal logo in corner.
- **Pricing tier cards:** `#1E1E1E` card background, Ember Orange tier name ("Early Bird"), Warm White details, teal IronPal logo.

## Color Scheme 3 — "Arctic Pulse" (Light & Clean)

### Palette

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Off-White | `#F7F8FC` | Page backgrounds, hero sections |
| **Secondary** | Cool Cloud | `#E8ECF1` | Card backgrounds, panels, dividers |
| **Accent** | Deep Teal | `#0A9C8E` | CTAs, links, active states, product highlights |
| **Accent 2** | Athletic Violet | `#6C5CE7` | Secondary accents — badges, graphs, data viz |
| **Text Primary** | Near Black | `#1A1A2E` | Headings, key text |
| **Text Secondary** | Cool Slate | `#6B7280` | Body text, captions |

### Visual Identity

- **Dominant feel:** Clean, airy, trustworthy. This scheme borrows from the modern health/wellness tech aesthetic (think Whoop, Oura Ring, Apple Health). White space dominates. Colors are used with surgical precision — teal for primary actions, violet for data/analytics accents. The feeling: this is a serious tech product backed by science, not a flashy gym gadget.
- **Typography:** Light-to-medium weight geometric sans-serif (Plus Jakarta Sans, Outfit, or General Sans). Generous letter spacing. The typography feels calm, modern, and considered.
- **Photography treatment:** Bright, clean gym shots with natural lighting. Light and airy — no heavy shadows. Skin tones natural and healthy. The IronPal product is the darkest element in every frame (matte black against bright environments), making it a focal anchor.
- **Gradients:** White-to-pale-blue vertical gradient backgrounds. Deep Teal-to-Athletic Violet gradient on featured CTA buttons for visual interest.

### Sample Applications

- **Kickstarter hero banner:** Off-white background, IronPal headband and cap on a light surface with soft shadows, product beauty shot with clean lines, Deep Teal "Back this project" CTA, violet data visualization graphic showing a workout log in the corner.
- **Social media post:** Light card, clean gym photo with natural light, athlete smiling in cap, teal text: "Zero-effort workout tracking." Violet metric badge: "87% accuracy and improving."
- **Infographic:** White background, Near Black headings, teal/violet data charts showing cost comparison, workout tracking accuracy, subscription tiers.

## Rationale & Recommendations

### Scheme 1 — "Stealth Teal" (Score: 8.40)

**Strengths:**
- Maximum product cohesion — the marketing *is* the product. The matte black + teal LED identity extends seamlessly from the physical hardware to every pixel of marketing. Backers see a unified, intentional brand.
- Dark-mode aesthetics are trending in both fitness and tech. The scheme immediately signals "premium tech product" without saying a word.
- The teal-as-only-color approach creates an instantly recognizable visual signature. In a Kickstarter scroll of colorful campaigns, a dark-and-teal page will stand out through contrast with the feed.

**Weaknesses:**
- Dark-mode-only limits versatility. Print materials, email clients with forced white backgrounds, and certain social platforms (LinkedIn post previews) may not render dark designs well.
- Extended dark-background reading can cause eye fatigue on information-heavy pages (FAQ sections, campaign updates, detailed specs).

**Best used for:** Kickstarter campaign page (hero + main body), video content (all three videos already use dark backgrounds), product packaging, and the core landing page. This is the scheme that should own the first impression.

**Marketing strategy alignment:** The Kickstarter hero video ("Your Workout, Logged") already lives in a dark visual world with warm gym lighting and teal accents. Stealth Teal makes the page and the video feel like one continuous brand experience.

---

### Scheme 2 — "Iron & Ember" (Score: 8.65 — Highest)

**Strengths:**
- The dual-accent system (ember for energy, teal for product) is the most visually dynamic of the three. It creates a clear information hierarchy: orange screams "action" (click here, buy now, limited time), while teal whispers "this is the product." That separation is a powerful UX tool for conversion-focused campaign pages.
- Highest fitness relevance score. The warm, high-contrast, athletic aesthetic is what the target audience (gym-goers, fitness enthusiasts) already responds to. It looks like it belongs alongside Nike, Under Armour, and Gymshark campaigns.
- The warm photography treatment makes athletes look their best — warm skin tones, dramatic shadows, golden gym lighting. This is the scheme that will make people *feel* something when they see the campaign.

**Weaknesses:**
- Two accent colors require more design discipline. Without careful application rules, materials can look busy or lose the clean, minimal quality that teal-only provides.
- The ember orange is further from the physical product's teal-only identity. Backers might perceive a slight disconnect between the warm marketing and the cool product.

**Best used for:** Social media advertising (paid Facebook/Instagram/TikTok ads), Kickstarter stretch goal announcements, email campaigns with urgency CTAs ("48 hours left!"), and the pricing/rewards section of the campaign page where conversion matters most.

**Marketing strategy alignment:** Video #6 ("The Numbers") is data-driven and fast-paced — the kinetic typography and urgency messaging pair perfectly with ember orange accents. The social media ad cuts from all three videos will perform better in feeds when framed with this warm, high-energy scheme.

---

### Scheme 3 — "Arctic Pulse" (Score: 8.05)

**Strengths:**
- Highest scores in versatility and accessibility. Light backgrounds work everywhere — web, email, print, pitch decks, partnership proposals. If IronPal needs to produce an investor deck, a partnership one-pager, or a press kit, Arctic Pulse is the scheme that looks professional and trustworthy in any context.
- The violet secondary accent adds a data/analytics personality that reinforces IronPal's AI-powered intelligence. It visually says: "This product doesn't just look cool — it's smart."
- Best text readability across all devices and environments. High contrast ratios pass WCAG AA standards effortlessly.

**Weaknesses:**
- Lowest fitness relevance. Light, airy, wellness-app aesthetics appeal to a health-conscious audience but may not resonate with hardcore gym-goers who prefer dark, aggressive visuals. There's a risk of looking like a meditation or yoga app rather than a strength training product.
- Less visual impact in social media feeds where thumb-stopping contrast is critical.

**Best used for:** Investor and partner-facing materials (pitch decks, press kits, B2B proposals), long-form campaign updates with heavy text, FAQ/support documentation, the IronPal app interface itself (dark mode optional, but light mode as default for usability), and any B2B interaction with gym owners.

**Marketing strategy alignment:** The "How It Works" explanatory content on the Kickstarter page benefits from clean, readable infographics. Arctic Pulse is the right scheme for the technical details section where clarity matters more than emotion.

## Recommended Multi-Scheme Strategy

Rather than choosing a single scheme, deploy all three strategically across different touchpoints:

| Touchpoint | Recommended Scheme | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Kickstarter hero section | Stealth Teal | Maximum impact, product-focused first impression |
| Kickstarter reward tiers / pricing | Iron & Ember | Urgency-driven CTAs, high conversion energy |
| Kickstarter "How It Works" section | Arctic Pulse | Clean readability for technical/data content |
| Video content (all 3 videos) | Stealth Teal | Already aligned with dark, cinematic video aesthetic |
| Paid social ads | Iron & Ember | Thumb-stopping warmth, athletic energy |
| Instagram/TikTok organic | Stealth Teal | Dark, moody, premium aesthetic for brand building |
| Investor pitch deck | Arctic Pulse | Professional, trustworthy, data-legible |
| Product packaging | Stealth Teal | Direct product identity match |
| Email campaigns | Iron & Ember (urgent) / Arctic Pulse (informational) | Match tone to email purpose |
| IronPal app UI | Stealth Teal (dark mode) / Arctic Pulse (light mode) | User preference toggle |

### Brand Hierarchy

1. **Stealth Teal** = Primary brand identity. The "default IronPal look."
2. **Iron & Ember** = Campaign/conversion mode. Used when the goal is action.
3. **Arctic Pulse** = Professional/informational mode. Used when the goal is clarity.

The unifying thread across all three: **Electric Teal (`#00E5CC`)** is always present as the IronPal product accent. It's the one color constant that ties every scheme back to the physical headband and cap.
