## Session summary

**Best take:** **Take 1 — `take_20260503-112515_20260503_111002.mp4`.** The lift at t≈6–9 s is the longest and highest-held of the three; the apex at t≈7.0 s is the cleanest single frame for the wordmark composite. Takes 2 and 3 are usable backups with shorter / lower holds.

**Reshoot priorities (in order, before tearing down the set today):**

1. **Prop orientation.** The headband is being lifted out as a coiled oval ring. For the wordmark composite to work in post, the long flat side of the band (the side that would be the wordmark face on the real product) must face camera at the apex. Pre-fold the prop before each take so it un-curls into a flat strip when lifted, not a loop. Mark the inside of the bag with tape so the prop resets the same way every take.
2. **Wardrobe.** Black is the right colour — keep it. **The white Mizuno bird on the chest must go**: gaffer-tape it (test that the tape edge isn't visible in the wide), turn the shirt inside-out (confirm no inner tag/print is visible), or buy a $10 plain matte-black crew. The bird is centred in the frame directly behind the prop at every apex.
3. **Backdrop.** Hang a charcoal or matte navy sheet/blanket 30 cm behind the bag to cover the corner shadow + smudges + bland wall colour. Highest visual-quality-per-effort change after the wardrobe fix.
4. **Lighting.** Add at least the warm key (a 3000 K lamp camera-left at 45° through a thin white sheet for diffusion). Even without bounce or rim, a directional warm key transforms the matte-black bag from "flat blob" into "premium fabric with specular edges".
5. **Tighten the takes** to 6–8 s. The lift only takes 2–3 s; trim the approach and exit.
6. **Audio is unfixable on this device** in the stock Camera app (verified). Either accept it (the editor strips it) or tape over the bottom-edge mic. Not worth a third-party-app learning curve unless you're recording in a noisy environment that distracts the actor.

**If only one of these can be addressed, fix the prop orientation (#1).** Issues #2 (wardrobe) and #3 (backdrop) are visually annoying but recoverable. A coiled prop, on the other hand, breaks the entire wordmark-composite plan that S3 is built around — the post pipeline assumes a flat face to track on.

## Process note (for future review-takes runs)

The original sparse 4-frame sampling at t=5/33/67/95 % systematically misses any event lasting under ~5 seconds in a 15-second take. The S3 lift specifically lives in the t=6–9 s window, which lands directly between t=33 % (~5 s) and t=67 % (~10 s) for these ~15 s takes. Future revisions of the `review-takes` skill should:

1. Either sample at a fixed 1-second cadence regardless of take duration (capped at ~30 frames per take to avoid runaway extraction), or
2. Detect "action moments" with motion-vector / scene-change probing (e.g. `ffmpeg -vf select='gt(scene,0.05)'`) and sample around those windows, or
3. After the sparse pass, automatically do a denser pass on the frame range where any actor body part is visible.

Logged here so the next iteration of the skill picks this up.
