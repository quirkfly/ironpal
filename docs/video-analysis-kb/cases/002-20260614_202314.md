## ✅ GROUND TRUTH (lifter-confirmed): BARBELL CURL (standing, supinated grip)

My final committed ID was **barbell upright row — WRONG**. Actual = **barbell curl**. The genus was
eventually right (standing arm exercise, bar rises thigh→chest, arm-driven), but I narrowed to upright
row on a **mis-read grip**.

**Score:** Exercise = **MISS** (predicted upright row; actual barbell curl). Reps/weight never scored.

**The decisive error — grip:** I called it **pronated (→ upright row)**; it was **supinated (→ curl)**.
Why I got it wrong:
- **Over-trusted the wristwatch** as a pronation landmark — watch-wearing side is ambiguous and I
  couldn't even resolve face-vs-strap on blurry frames.
- **Asserted "knuckles face the camera" (cue B) — but under magnification there were ZERO visible
  knuckles**; the surface was a smooth palm/heel. I claimed a feature I could not actually resolve.
- Read grips off **motion-blurred, edge-of-frame, rotated** frames and forced a conclusion.
- Also likely mis-read "elbows flare out" (→ upright row); a curl keeps elbows pinned.

**THE LESSON (banked widely): verify a grip by RESOLVING palm-vs-knuckles — count the knuckles / look
for palm creases. If you cannot see distinct knuckles, do NOT claim the back of the hand. Never infer
pronation from a wristwatch, and never assert a grip feature on a frame too blurry/edge/rotated to
resolve it.** Also: this whole case flip-flopped (loading→deadlift→curl-family→upright row→CURL) — the
through-line failure was *claiming features I couldn't actually see*.
