### The decisive error — I misread a CABLE MACHINE as a free-weight barbell deadlift
Every "smoking gun" I cited for a deadlift was actually the **cable rig**:
- The **"long black bar held both-hands pronated"** = the **straight-bar PUSHDOWN ATTACHMENT**, not a barbell.
- The **"chain"** and the **"thin draw-wire to a floor anchor"** = the **CABLE/CHAIN of the pulley
  running from the bar to the weight** — the *defining feature of a cable exercise*. I saw it three
  separate times and labelled it "accommodating-resistance chain" and "string-potentiometer
  instrumentation tether" — never "this is a cable machine."
- The **"DOMYOS plates being loaded"** = plates loaded onto the cable's **weight pin/carrier**, not a bar.
- The **GymBeam belt** = ambient; irrelevant.
- I never found a **floor↔hip trajectory** (correctly!) — because there is none. A pushdown is
  **arm-driven elbow EXTENSION, person stationary, head-still**. I forced deadlift-vs-row instead of
  asking whether it was a free-weight lift at all.

### THE LESSON (the real generalizable fix): a flexible CABLE/CHAIN from the held bar/handle to a
### weight stack / loading pin / pulley ⇒ a CABLE-MACHINE exercise (pushdown/row/pulldown), NOT a
### barbell/dumbbell lift. **Check what the implement CONNECTS to before naming any free-weight lift.**
Rigid bar with fixed loaded ends + NO cable = free weight. Bar/handle + a flexible cable/chain to a
load = cable. The cable is the #1 identifier and I must trace it FIRST. (Banked in
exercise-identification.md + skill; corrects the WRONG "draw-wire = instrumented deadlift" lesson I
committed in d1a49f9.)
