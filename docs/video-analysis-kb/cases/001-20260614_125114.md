## Diagnosis & rules added (after reveal)

**Exercise — HIT.** Blind call was curl at 0.40 (correct but barely above coin-toss); the KB v2
proximity/"looming" cue lifted it to 0.62 *before* reveal, and it was right. The cues that earned it:
1. **Looming/apparent-size** — dumbbell large & close, hugging the body, never out at arm's length.
   **Validated on real footage** → this is now the #1 cue in `dumbbell-biceps-curl.md` /
   `dumbbell-shoulder-raise.md` and it carried the call.
2. Bottom-of-rep out of frame toward the hip (ruled out press).
3. Top near face, not overhead (ruled out press).

**What this confirms for the KB:**
- For head-still arm pairs, **switching from apparent-height to apparent-size/proximity is what
  moves the call from a guess to a correct answer** — generalizable, keep leading with it.
- The **0.62 ceiling was honest**: it reflected the egocentric blind spot (never saw my own elbow).
  Reality matched, but the product needs IMU rep-clock + arm-landmarking to push this past 0.9.

**Correction (rep-counting pass):** it's **two dumbbells, one per hand** (t=24 pickup, t=60 setdown
both show two), NOT one-arm. Exercise still "biceps curl" ✅; the "one-arm/right" qualifier was wrong.

**Reps — could not count accurately from video (honest miss).** Method: count "looming" cycles
(dumbbell rises close to lens = top, drops out = bottom) on tiled montages. Failure modes hit:
1. **Aliasing-vs-legibility bind:** 4fps montage (count_01, 8×16) is dense enough not to alias but
   tiles are ~37px = unreadable; 2fps (reps_01) is readable but can miss fast turnarounds. No single
   montage is both.
2. **Simultaneous vs alternating (2×/÷2 confound):** with two dumbbells, simultaneous curls → each
   looming = 1 rep; alternating → looming events double, per-arm count halves. Unresolvable from
   these blurry frames.
3. Head-bob vs rep; pickup/setdown contamination at the set boundaries.
Best estimate: set ≈26–60s; **~10–12 reps if simultaneous, ~8–9/arm if alternating**, conf ~0.4.
