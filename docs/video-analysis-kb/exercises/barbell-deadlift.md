# Barbell deadlift (conventional)

**Equipment:** loaded barbell, lifted from the floor. **Status:** active — case 002 (~0.75, pending
ground truth). Prime movers: glutes, hamstrings, spinal erectors (+ lats, traps, forearms, quads).

## How it's performed
1. **Setup:** feet ~hip-width, **mid-foot under the bar** (bar near shins); hinge down, grip just
   outside the legs (double-overhand/mixed); hips above knees, **flat/neutral back**, chest up, lats
   engaged, shins to bar; brace the core.
2. **Pull:** drive through the floor with the legs ("push the floor away"); bar stays on the shins,
   travels a **straight vertical line close to the body**; hips & shoulders rise together, then drive
   hips forward once the bar clears the knees.
3. **Lockout:** stand fully upright, bar at upper thighs/hips, shoulders back, glutes squeezed (no
   lean-back).
4. **Descent:** push hips back first (hinge), bar close; bend knees after it passes them; lower to
   the floor. One rep = floor → standing lockout → floor.

Form: neutral spine throughout; vertical bar path; hips don't shoot up first.

## Equipment trajectory (for Step 6b template match)
**Floor → up shins/thighs (vertical, close to body) → hip-height at standing lockout → reverse to
floor.** Bar held **in the hands** throughout; **rests on the floor** at the bottom of each rep.

Falsifiers vs neighbours (case 002 verification):
- **RDL:** bar **never touches the floor** (starts/ends at hip, hinge to mid-shin).
- **Bent-over row:** torso **stays hinged**, person **never stands**, bar pulled **to the torso**, off floor.
- **Squat:** bar at **shoulders/back**, not hanging in the hands; never near floor.
- **Power clean:** ends at the **shoulders** (front rack), explosive — deadlift ends at the **hip**.

## Grip / forearm check (distinguishes a deadlift hold from HANDLING the bar)
A deadlift hold = **both hands** on a **horizontal** bar, **arms hanging dead-straight down from the
shoulders** so the **forearms are vertical and perpendicular to the bar**, grip pronated/mixed and
**passive** (hands are hooks; arms never bend — hips/knees do the work). If you see **one hand**, a
**forearm angled to the bar**, or an actively-flexed/holding arm → that's **handling/carrying the
bar, not a deadlift rep** (case 002 t56 error: I called a one-hand angled-forearm hold a "lockout"
three times). **Read the grip/forearms FIRST; don't reinterpret a frame to fit the active conclusion.**

## Egocentric (headband) signature
- **Bottom:** head looks down at the bar near the floor; feet/floor **large & close** (deep hinge).
- **Top:** standing, bar at the **hips**; more room visible.
- Strong **vertical head translation** bottom↔top → a head-moving exercise → **IMU rep-counts it
  cleanly** (unlike head-still arm work). Loaded-bar judgement: plates at the bar ENDS (often
  off-frame) — see weight-reading.md (bare center ≠ empty; state persists across frames).
