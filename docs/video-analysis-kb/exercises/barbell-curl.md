# Barbell curl (standing biceps curl)

**Equipment:** barbell (here a loaded DOMYOS spinlock bar), standing.
**Status:** active — **lifter-confirmed ID for case 002** (after I wrongly called it an upright row).
Prime movers: biceps brachii, brachialis.

## How it's performed
Standing, **supinated (underhand) grip** — **palms UP / toward the body**, ~shoulder-width. Elbows
**pinned at the sides** (upper arm fixed); the **forearms rotate up**, curling the bar from the thighs
up to the **shoulders/upper chest**, then lowered back to the thighs. Bar stays close; **never touches
the floor**.

## The decisive cue vs upright row (case 002 lesson)
Curl and upright row **both raise the bar thigh→chest** — they are NOT separated by the trajectory.
They are separated by **GRIP + elbows**:
- **Curl:** **supinated (palm up)** — you see the **PALM / finger-pads, NO knuckle row** — elbows
  **pinned**.
- **Upright row:** **pronated (palm down)** — you can **count ~4 knuckles** on the back of the hand —
  elbows **flare up/out**.
**To call it, RESOLVE palm-vs-knuckles by zooming** (count knuckles vs see palm); do NOT infer the
grip from a wristwatch and do NOT assert knuckles you can't see. (Case 002: I mis-read supinated as
pronated and called upright row — MISS. See `exercise-identification.md`.)

## Egocentric (headband) signature
Standing, head down. Bar at thighs (bottom, arms extended) → curls up to the shoulders/upper chest
(elbows pinned, forearms rotating up). Same bar-rise as an upright row → **grip is the only reliable
discriminator from a head cam**.
