# Bulgarian split squat

**Equipment:** dumbbells (one per hand) or bodyweight; rear foot elevated on a bench.
**Status:** seeded from POC (egocentric); not yet validated against a real KB clip.

## Visual signature

- **Third-person:** split stance, rear foot up on a bench, front knee tracks over the toe, torso
  near-upright, vertical up/down driven by the front leg. Clear, distinctive stance.
- **Egocentric (headband):** you mostly see hands/dumbbells and the **floor rising and falling** in
  a deep vertical range; a bench may pass through the lower/rear frame on the descent. Larger
  vertical head travel than most standing exercises.

## Rep definition

One rep = front-leg descent (hip/knee flexion, floor rises) → drive up to near-lockout (floor
falls). Count completed up-phases. Single-leg: a full set is one side; note if the clip is one side
or both.

## Weight reading

Dumbbell head numbers — see `weight-reading.md`. Catch the frame at the bottom/top where a head
faces the camera. Often bodyweight in early POC clips → "not applicable".

## Confusions to rule out

- Walking lunges (travelling, no fixed rear-foot bench).
- Step-ups (front foot elevated, not rear).
- Regular squat (symmetrical stance, both feet grounded).
