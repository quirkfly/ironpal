# Barbell upright row

**Equipment:** barbell (here a loaded DOMYOS spinlock bar), standing.
**Status:** reference signature only. ⚠️ It was WRONGLY assigned to case 002 — case 002 was actually a
**barbell CURL** (supinated grip), mis-read as an upright row because I called the grip pronated when
it was supinated. Keep this file as a general upright-row reference, NOT as a case-002 example. Prime
movers: lateral/anterior deltoid, traps; upper-back/biceps assist.

**Upright row vs curl — the deciding cue is GRIP (both raise the bar thigh→chest):** upright row =
**pronated/overhand** (palm down) + elbows flare up/out, bar to chin; curl = **supinated/underhand**
(palm up) + elbows pinned, bar to shoulder. Resolve the grip by palm-vs-knuckles (see
`exercise-identification.md`); do NOT guess it from a wristwatch.

## How it's performed
Standing, **pronated (overhand)** grip ~shoulder-width or narrower. The bar travels **from the thighs
straight up the front of the body to the chin / upper chest**, with the **elbows LEADING — flaring
up and out to the sides** above the bar — then lowered back to the thighs. Bar stays close to the body,
**never touches the floor**.

## Disambiguation cues (how case 002 was settled)
- **Grip pronated** → NOT a biceps curl (which is supinated). [grip_t53]
- **Elbows flare OUT to the sides; forearms run horizontal-outward** from the bar to the elbows at
  the top → NOT a reverse curl (elbows stay pinned, forearms vertical-in-front). [top_t58]
- **Bar endpoint high (chin/upper chest)** → above a curl's chest-level top. [top_t60/63 out of view]

## Distinguish from
- **Biceps / reverse curl:** elbows **pinned at the sides**, forearms vertical in front, bar to
  chest; curl is supinated, reverse curl pronated.
- **Deadlift / RDL:** arms **straight & passive**, bar stays **at/below the hips** and returns to the
  floor; upright row raises the bar **above the hips via elbow flexion** (arm-driven, bar to chin).
- **Front raise:** straight arms, bar out at arm's length forward; upright row keeps the bar close
  with bent elbows.

## Egocentric (headband) signature
Standing, head down. Bar at thighs (bottom) rises **up the front of the body**; at the top it goes
**high enough to leave the downward view** (chin level), with the **elbows winging out to the frame
edges**. Identify by reading the **trajectory direction (thigh→chin), grip (pronated), and elbow path
(flaring out)** — never from the thigh pose alone (it's shared with deadlift/curl). See
`exercise-identification.md` (verify trajectory direction + forearm/elbow).
