# Dumbbell biceps curl

**Equipment:** one or two dumbbells, standing (or seated). Single-arm or both.
**Status:** active — primary call on case 001 (one-arm, right; conf ~0.62, pre-reveal).

## Biomechanics

- **Pivot: elbow.** Upper arm stays **vertical and pinned to the torso**; only the forearm moves.
- Elbow angle goes ~160–170° (bottom) → ~40–50° (top).
- Hand traces a **short arc that hugs the body**; dumbbell ends near the **shoulder/chest, close to
  the torso**.
- Grip: supinated (standard) / neutral (hammer) / pronated (reverse) — elbow flexion is constant.
- Prime movers: biceps brachii, brachialis, brachioradialis.

## Egocentric (headband) signature — ranked by what the camera can actually see

1. **"Looming" — apparent size / proximity (best cue):** because the weight hugs the body and passes
   ~30–50 cm from the lens, it appears **large and looming**, entering from the **bottom/near side**,
   growing as it rises, briefly **occluding the near background**, with a **big apparent-size change
   (strong parallax)**. Ends high & close near the face/chest; **never small/distant out over the
   room**. This monocular depth cue is the most robust thing a head cam reads. ← used to call case 001.
2. **Bottom of rep:** dumbbell drops low, **out of frame toward the hip** (arm extended at side).
3. Forearm **supination** if the hand is catchable (palm rotates toward face).
4. Elbow flexion — cleanest in theory but usually an **egocentric blind spot** (own elbow rarely in
   frame); use arm-landmarking / wide FOV to recover.

Contrast: a curl makes the weight **loom huge and close**; a raise keeps it **small and out over the
room** (`dumbbell-shoulder-raise.md`). Caveat: this is depth inferred from monocular size — fragile
with tiny dumbbells / odd stance; the product's IMU rep-clock reads the apex frame where the size
contrast is maximal.

## Distinguish from

- **Shoulder raise** (`dumbbell-shoulder-raise.md`): raise keeps the arm **straight** and the weight
  goes to **arm's length** — proximity is the decider.
- **Shoulder press:** weight goes **overhead**, head usually tilts up; curl tops near face, not over.
- **One-arm row:** bent-over torso, weight pulled to the ribcage — curl is upright.
